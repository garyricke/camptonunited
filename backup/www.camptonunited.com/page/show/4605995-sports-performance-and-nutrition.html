Sports Performance & Nutrition

The main goal of the Campton Sports Performance Program is to provide our players the means by which they can improve on-field performance. 

We want to create a challenging training environment that will push each player to increase their overall athleticism while reducing the risk of injuries through a systematic approach. As the game continues to evolve and the speed of play gets faster, we want to give our players the athletic edge that will put them over the top during competitions. Ultimately, we want to maximize the athletic potential of each player so they can perform to the best of their ability.

Through the use of free weights, suspension trainers, resistance bands, medicine balls, plyo-boxes and agility equipment our main focus is to:

-Improve Strength

-Improve Power

-Reduce Risk of Injury

-Develop Functional Fitness and Skill by replicating soccer specific movements

Our coaches are trained and certified in teaching and assessing proper movements to reduce the risk of injury. Our focus is quality over quantity and establishing good form and technique throughout the workouts to help players build good habits. Each piece of equipment will help develop our players through proper instruction. Our equipment is safe and easy to use, regardless of experience.

Through the use of the Hex-Bar, which uses 90% of the athlete's musculature, we will be looking to improve peak ground force which directly correlates with speed and acceleration. Because of its shape, the hex-bar makes it easier to keep a good balance and maintain good spinal position so it's safe for athletes who are new to lifting.

Our TRX-Suspension Trainers will help build full-body strength and balance by using a percentage of the athlete's body weight and allowing them to move through various angles in order to increase or decrease the difficulty of the exercise. We will be utilizing plyometric exercises in combination with resistance bands and medicine balls to develop power in all planes of motion. Soccer is very demanding in the sense that players have to sprint, stop and turn in all different directions. We will address the correct technique of acceleration and deceleration as well as proper arm drive. We will be bringing some of these elements together in which we will incorporate the ball while continuing to work on footwork, balance, power, coordination and body control. 

Although team training sessions are integral to team building and are mandatory as part of Campton curriculum, the success of the individual will be based on his/her commitment. We want every player to be committed to their development and approach every session with the focus and drive to get better! 

The Hex Bar Deadlift – Why we do it

The Hex Bar Deadlift – Why we do it

Written by Coach Marc and Coach Cesar

Our SAQ program is designed to decrease the potential for injury as well as to improve each players’ speed, explosive power, and strength. While most of our program is based on bodyweight movements, plyometrics, and mini-band work, the cornerstone of our strength training element is the Hex-Bar Deadlift. When designing a program for an entire group spanning many ages, we have to factor in these variables:

  • Risk vs Reward
  • Time Restrictions
  • Training Experience
  • Individual Athleticism

The Hex Bar Deadlift is the perfect lift to load the athlete without unnecessarily risking injury.

  1. The Hex Bar Deadlift is extremely safe

Has your child ever picked a box up off of the floor? When they did this, did you supervise them the entire time? Did you analyze their head placement, squat angle and if they were lifting in a balanced manner? When doing the Hex-Bar Deadlift, we are simulating a Primal Lift – or a lift one would do in nature as a primitive human. The Hex Bar Deadlift is the primal movement of “bending”, and is very natural and safe. The Campton staff emphasize key components like “neutral spine” and safety is always number 1. Plus, with the Hex-Bar Deadlift, we are lifting in a neutral position, unlike a conventional deadlift where you have a forward lean which place unnecessary stress on the lower back. With the Hex-Bar Deadlift, we are able to load the posterior-chain (the posterior chain are the muscles that generate force during running located on the backside of the body such as hamstrings and erectors) directly and avoid any chance being unbalanced. 

  1. The learning curve is fast

The Hex-Bar Deadlift, if taught correctly, can be perfected in one session. If we were to use lifts like the Snatch, it would take weeks to even learn the form, and even that wouldn’t be optimal form.

  1. Bang for the Buck

The Hex-Bar Deadlift is estimated to work over 90% of a person’s musculature. Hence, we can get more workload done in this one movement versus 10 other movements combined.

  1. Science is Speed, Speed is Science

According to data compiled by research funded by Nike®, the Hex-Bar Deadlift was the ONLY gym exercise to be directly correlated to increased speed. Other data shows the Hex-Bar Deadlift offers superior benefits to power generation based on its generation of data from the legs and not lower back but has all of the benefits of the deadlift – like increased posterior-chain load. (1) 

  1. Injury Prevention

The better the body will be able to handle controlled loads, like a Hex-Bar Deadlift, the better it will be able to handle an uncontrolled impact, like a hard tackle on the field.


Take-Home Message

At Campton, we want to develop humans, not just athletes. The movements we do will help set forth your child-athlete for a life of a healthy mind and body and to prevent future injuries. While these will have an immediate impact on their play, the life-long impact is our main objective.

Why we use Mini-Bands

Why we use Mini-Bands

By: Coach Marc Lobliner


A large part of our SAQ work involves mini-bands. Mini-bands are small resistance bands that go around your legs, typically around the ankle or above the knee. These are critical in not only developing strength and power in the lower body but also in activating the gluteal muscles, aka the buttocks, to absorb some of the impacts that the knee, namely the ACL, will encounter during normal soccer activity. 


These movements stimulate the neurological system to engage the glutes into activities. By doing these movements routinely, it will become second nature for your athlete to utilize these muscles during play. Weak or underutilized glutes can result in poor alignment of the hips, knees, and ankles forcing the knees to absorb the impact. This can cause damage to the ACL over the long-term and also increase the chances of an impact injury, such as an ACL-tear from a pivot or a tackle.

If you ever witness our sessions at Campton United, you will hear me say repeatedly, “Drop your butt, chest out, eyes straight ahead, work your arms!” This mimics the position used in play, so again, nature takes over and proper form greatly reduces the risk of injury. By doing sport-specific movement CORRECTLY, repeatedly, our muscles are ready to fire properly in the heat of battle.

At Campton United, we want long-term success and health for your youth-athletes. We want to be transparent with all of the methods used to ensure the safest and most complete development for our athletes.

Why We Use Mini-Hurdles

Why We Use Mini-Hurdles

By: Coach Marc Lobliner         

Every day, new studies surface about how to create better athletes. We love these, and in this article,  we will delve into why mini-hurdles are great for increasing speed and explosive acceleration. Remember, these are Campton United’s top priorities with the SAQ program IN ORDER:

  1. PREVENT INJURIES
  2. Get Faster and Stronger

You cannot get faster and stronger if you are injured and you darn sure cannot play. We focus on many variables when programming for what your youth-athlete will perform that day. For instance, a 2017 study showing significantly reduced risk of hamstring injuries in collegiate sprinters that incorporated strength, agility and flexibility training.(1)  That’s right, not just ONE training method, but many will help prevent injuries. We use Mini-Hurdles for this very reason, to not only improve performance but to keep your child healthy and strong….and on the field!

Mini-Hurdles are literally that, very small hurdles that we do both bilateral (both legs), unilateral (one leg), with forward and lateral “hops” to help:

  1. Train the athlete to support their entire limb including the knee and ankle joint when off-balance. This is critical in preventing both ankle and knee injuries.
  2. Mini-hurdles can help to increase your stride length and lower body power so there will be both greater acceleration and absolute speed.
  3. By balancing with the ankles and activating the calves and tibialis, the mini-hurdles allow the athlete to gain greater control over these extremities, thus being able to activate them greater in a speed-required situation.
  4. Balance is critical when executing moves. There are very few drills that promote balance as much as mini-hurdles. 
  5. If you are ever near an SAQ session, you will hear me say, “Focus on driving your arms and the landing!” Arm drive is critical in maximizing speed, and these drills train the athlete to always utilize both the backward and forward swing of the arms to generate power. By focusing on the landing, we ensure the athlete is trained to land properly with both one leg and both legs even when unbalanced to avoid injury.

The long-term health benefits for your child’s physical wellness and quality of life are of the utmost importance to us. This tool is one of many things we will use to not only bring out the best in your child now but also to make them better and more functional humans for the rest of their lives.
